Peregrine

00 Project Overview

Tools

  • Figma
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Adobe Illustrator

Role

  • UX designer

Peregrine is a desktop app allowing users to manage their health record and communicate with their medical team. Peregrine also allows users to build and save exercise routines, mindfulness practices, and personalized goals. The app is designed for people looking to more closely track their medical data and incorporate exercise/mindfulness into their overall health and wellness.

01 Exploring the Problem

Problem Statement

Users need a fast, confidential and efficient means to access, record, review, update and search their health history and fitness/wellness habits and routines because their time is stretched thin from other responsibilities. We will know this to beÂ true when Peregrine has 1000 regular users using the health tracking, fitness tracking and/or mindfulness features at least once a day.

02 Building an Understanding of the User

Meet Brooke

Needs:

  1. A workout tracker and goal setting features that facilitate her conditioning prior to her upcoming half marathon.
  2. A place to find new exercise ideas for herself and her clients.
  3. A medical record interface that both she and her doctor can manage.
  4. A food diary / nutrition tracker to facilitate healthy diet choices for managing her type I diabetes

05 Usability Testing

Once early wireframes were done and clickable prototypes were built, it was time to start getting users in front of the product for testing.

Participants: 06

Mean task success rate: 78%

Mean SEQ: 5.46 / 7.00

Key Insights

01 Sidebar navigation was not clear. Users would rarely click the correct part of the sidebar

02 Text too small

Redesign of the sidebar to include profile image, greeting and expandable headings.

Key Insights Continued

03 Users couldn't find 'workout library' from 'add exercises screen'.

So I redesigned the 'add exercises' screen completely into a drag and drop format. This redesign also placed the call to action buttons together and in places that are consistent with the rest of the app.

07 Reflection

01 There were a number of constraints on the utility of this project as it stand now, chief among them being this was built for an academic project and was prompted from that institution.

02 I would have wanted to spend more time doing user research and understanding the problem space before moving into building solutions.

03 As I reflect on the project now, I see that data-driven decision making is paramount to designing a product that users will be willing to pay for. This also means that as UX designer, I will need to know the facts, use the facts to make decisions, and eliminate the personal bias that I carry as a designer.
